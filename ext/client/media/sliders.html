Energy Contextual Examples

Energy Range Description & Examples
< 1 J Very small energy, typical in fine mechanical or electronic systems.
Electricity: Powering a microcontroller for a few milliseconds.
Impact: A feather falling from 1 meter.
Heat: Touching a surface briefly without feeling warmth.
1 J Enough to move a small object.
Impact: Lifting a 100g object 1 meter.
Electricity: A 1W LED running for 1 second.
Heat: Click of a retractable pen.
1–10 J Energy in small mechanical movements.
Impact: Snapping a rubber band.
Electricity: Powering a smartwatch for a few seconds.
Heat: Heating 1g of water by ≈2°C..
10–100 J Noticeable force or electrical use.
Impact: A strong punch (≈100 J).
Electricity: A 10W LED for 10 seconds.
Mechanical: Dropping a 1 kg object from 1 meter height (10 J).
100 J – 1 kJ Significant energy in daily actions.
Impact: Dropping a 10 kg object from 1 meter.
Electricity: A 100W bulb for 10 seconds.
Safety: EN ISO 20345-rated toe caps can absorb 100–200 J.
1–10 kJ Energy in daily activities.
Impact: Kinetic energy of a moving bicycle.
Electricity: Boiling 100 ml of water (≈8.4 kJ).
Food: A small snack (≈1 kcal = 4.2 kJ).
10–100 kJ Large tools or explosives.
Impact: Hammering or industrial stamping machines.
Electricity: Microwave use for ≈30 seconds.
Explosives: 1g of TNT releases ≈4.2 kJ.
100 kJ – 1 MJ Household appliances and vehicle motion.
Heating: Boiling 1 liter of water (≈ 420 kJ). Electricity: Running a microwave for 1–2 minutes.
Mechanical: Starting an electric car motor.
1–10 MJ Everyday Energy Use.
Electricity: A 12W bulb running for ≈9.6 days (1 MJ).
Electricity: Running a 1kW heater for over an hour.
Daily: Human brain activity per day (≈5-6 MJ).
